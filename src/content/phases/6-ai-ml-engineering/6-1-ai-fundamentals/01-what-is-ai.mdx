## Why this matters

AI is the defining technology of this decade. But the gap between what the media says AI is and what engineers actually build is enormous. This lesson draws the real map — the one you need to navigate the job market, pick the right tools, and avoid the hype traps.

## The nested circles

Three terms, three scopes:

- **Artificial Intelligence** — the broadest field. Any system that performs tasks normally requiring human intelligence. Includes rule-based systems, search algorithms, and planning — things that have nothing to do with neural networks.
- **Machine Learning** — a subset of AI. Systems that learn patterns from data instead of following hardcoded rules. Decision trees, SVMs, random forests, and neural networks all live here.
- **Deep Learning** — a subset of ML. Neural networks with many layers. This is where LLMs, image generators, and speech recognition live.

```text
AI ⊃ ML ⊃ Deep Learning ⊃ LLMs
```

When someone says "AI" in 2026, they almost always mean LLMs. That is technically imprecise but practically useful — LLMs are where most of the engineering jobs are.

## The 2026 landscape

The foundation model ecosystem has settled into tiers:

**Closed-weight frontier models:** Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI (GPT), Google (Gemini). You access them via API. You cannot see or modify the weights. They lead on reasoning and safety.

**Open-weight models:** Meta (Llama), Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek. You can download the weights, run them locally, fine-tune them. Smaller, cheaper, but increasingly competitive.

**The shift:** The industry has moved from "who can train the biggest model" to "who can build the best product on top of models." Training is a capital-intensive race between a few labs. Inference, tooling, and application engineering is where most engineers work.

## What an AI engineer does

The day-to-day of an AI engineer in 2026 is mostly plumbing, not math:

- Designing prompts and testing them systematically
- Building RAG pipelines (retrieve documents, stuff into context)
- Wiring tool use so models can take actions
- Building evaluation harnesses to measure quality
- Optimizing cost (model routing, caching, prompt compression)
- Handling errors, rate limits, and streaming

You will write more JSON schemas than gradient descent equations.

## What you learned

- AI ⊃ ML ⊃ Deep Learning ⊃ LLMs. When people say "AI" in 2026, they usually mean LLMs.
- The landscape has frontier closed models (Claude, GPT, Gemini) and open-weight alternatives (Llama, Mistral).
- AI engineering is mostly plumbing: prompts, RAG, tool use, evaluation, and cost management.
